Why care about strong passwords?

Your digital keychain (that is, your collection of passwords) dictate who can access which of your things over the internet. An easily-guessable password or a password you share across different accounts is a vulnerable one.

But why care about this? If a bad actor accesses your password, they'll be able to log in to any accounts associated with it--including things like your bank account.

Strong passwords are your first line of defense to prevent this. The longer a password is, the more difficult it is for a hacker to crack it.

Essentially, passwords are stored using an algorithm known as hashing. Unlike encryption, hashing is not reversible. So, the only way to recover hashed passwords is by comparing the hashed forms of all possible candidates until there is a match. Thus, the longer and more character types a password has, the longer it will take to brute force it.

How to make a strong password

As you can see, the length of a password is the most important factor for its strength. However, it's also critical to use a mix of lower and uppercase letters, numbers, and symbols.

This is because the longer a password is and the greater variety of characters that it uses, the longer it takes to brute-force as there is a greater amount of potential characters in each spot of a password.
